Abstract

Vision-Language-Action driving models adapt pretrained VLMs into trajectory planners, gaining world knowledge and language-following ability. Yet current VLA driving systems remain limited by weak visual geometry modeling, sparse expert coverage of safety-critical cues, and insufficient support for multimodal trajectory outputs.

DriveStack-VLA addresses these limitations with a BEV-enhanced VLM decoder, render-teacher supervision for perceptual grounding, GRPO-based reward alignment, and a lightweight self-critique module that scores sampled futures and conditionally refines the selected trajectory.

Architecture

The actor generates action tokens from camera, BEV, instruction, and ego-state inputs. The score head reuses visual hidden states to rank candidate trajectories and conditionally refine the selected plan.

Actor

Multilevel Visual Injection

Camera and BEV tokens are projected into the VLM decoder through a DeepStack-style pathway, allowing multiple decoder blocks to access scene geometry while producing action-token sequences.

Score

Candidate Ranking

The scoring head uses the final visual hidden states and candidate trajectories to estimate trajectory quality, enabling best-of-K selection without expensive text generation.

Refinement

Residual Correction

When the top candidate falls below a confidence threshold, the refinement head predicts bounded residuals to improve the trajectory while preserving decoder efficiency.

Three-Stage Training Recipe

The training pipeline separates visual grounding, reward alignment, and efficient inference-time trajectory selection.

1

Render-Teacher Alignment

Stage 1 aligns real-camera perception with rasterized render-teacher signals through masked camera-token alignment and action-to-vision attention distillation.

2

Reward-Driven Candidate Generation

Stage 2 applies GRPO-style fine-tuning with a joint reward: trajectory-space driving quality encourages safe proposals, while token-space format reward keeps outputs strictly decodable.

3

Self-Critique Heads

Stage 3 freezes the actor and trains compact scoring and refinement heads, turning multimodal sampling into a fast score-select-refine process.
