DriveStack-VLA

Render-Teacher Alignment and Self-Critique for Vision-Language-Action Driving

Anonymous Authors

Overview Architecture Training Visual Evidence Results Candidate Reasoning

DriveStack-VLA trains a VLA driving policy in three stages: visual grounding through Render-Teacher Alignment, reward-driven candidate generation, and lightweight self-critique for scoring and refinement.

What This Work Solves

VLA driving models inherit strong language and world knowledge from vision-language backbones, but they still struggle with geometry-aware perception, sparse expert cues, and multimodal trajectory choice. DriveStack-VLA addresses these issues by combining BEV geometry, render-based visual supervision, and a self-critique module that can rank and refine sampled trajectories.

Geometry

BEV DeepStack Injection

A top-down BEV branch is injected into the language-model decoder so action tokens receive explicit geometric context in addition to front-view visual features.

Alignment

Render-Teacher Signals

Rasterized render views guide camera-token alignment and action-to-vision attention, encouraging the policy to focus on drivable lanes, nearby vehicles, and safety-critical regions.

Reasoning

Score, Select, Refine

Instead of trusting a single decoded trajectory, the model samples candidates, scores them with a compact critic, and applies residual refinement when confidence is insufficient.

Architecture

The actor generates action tokens from camera, BEV, instruction, and ego-state inputs. The critic reuses visual hidden states to rank candidate trajectories and conditionally refine the selected plan.

Actor

Multilevel Visual Injection

Camera and BEV tokens are projected into the VLM decoder through a DeepStack-style pathway, allowing multiple decoder blocks to access scene geometry while producing action-token sequences.

Critic

Candidate Ranking

The scoring head uses the final visual hidden states and candidate trajectories to estimate trajectory quality, enabling best-of-K selection without expensive text generation.

Refinement

Residual Correction

When the top candidate falls below a confidence threshold, the refinement head predicts bounded residuals to improve the trajectory while preserving decoder efficiency.

Three-Stage Training Recipe

The training pipeline separates visual grounding, reward alignment, and efficient inference-time trajectory selection.

1

Render-Teacher Alignment

Stage 1 aligns real-camera perception with rasterized render-teacher signals through masked camera-token alignment and action-to-vision attention distillation.

2

Reward-Driven Candidate Generation

Stage 2 applies GRPO-style fine-tuning with a joint reward: trajectory-space driving quality encourages safe proposals, while token-space format reward keeps outputs strictly decodable.

3

Self-Critique Heads

Stage 3 freezes the actor and trains compact scoring and refinement heads, turning multimodal sampling into a fast score-select-refine process.

Visual Evidence

These visualizations show the model improving both trajectory quality and visual focus, rather than only reporting aggregate scores.

Trajectory Quality Improves Across Stages

The backbone tends to deviate under dense traffic or degraded perception, while the proposed stages produce safer, lane-consistent trajectories.

Attention Moves Toward Safety-Critical Cues

Render-teacher supervision guides action tokens to attend to drivable structure and nearby objects, making the visual grounding more relevant for planning.

Small Candidate Sets Are Enough

Scoring plus refinement gives consistent gains while saturating early, reaching a practical operating point with a small number of sampled candidates.
