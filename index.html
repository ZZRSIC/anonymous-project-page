Abstract

Vision-Language-Action driving models convert a pretrained Vision-Language Model into a driving policy, allowing them to use world knowledge and follow language goals. DriveStack-VLA addresses weak visual geometry modeling, insufficient scenario diversity, incomplete perceptual cues in expert demonstrations, and limited support for multimodal trajectory outputs.

The framework injects a Bird-Eye-View representation into the language-model decoder through a multilevel pathway, aligns real-image perception with rasterized render-teacher signals, and uses reinforcement fine-tuning with self-critique to select and refine sampled trajectories.

Method Overview

Stage 1

Render-Teacher Alignment

Aligns real-image perceptual focus with rasterized render-teacher signals, strengthening geometric and safety-critical cues before policy alignment.

Stage 2

GRPO-Based RFT

Samples candidate action-token sequences and optimizes them with a joint reward for trajectory quality and strict format consistency.

Stage 3

Self-Critique

Scores candidate trajectories, selects the best plan, and conditionally refines a candidate when the confidence threshold is not satisfied.

Frozen Reference KL

Stage-2 keeps the actor close to the supervised Stage-1 prior by regularizing each sampled candidate against a frozen reference policy initialized from the SFT checkpoint.

D_KL ~= r_ref - log(r_ref) - 1

Joint Reward

The driving reward averages normalized NC, TTC, and DAC scores. The format reward combines length, token-range, and schema terms to preserve strict decodability.

r = r_driving + alpha_fmt * r_fmt

GRPO Training Loop

  1. Sample an observation and generate a group of candidate action-token sequences.
  2. Decode format-valid candidates and compute driving rewards.
  3. Compute the format reward for every sampled sequence.
  4. Normalize rewards within the group to obtain relative advantages.
  5. Apply the clipped GRPO objective with reference-policy KL regularization.

Implementation Details

The BEV encoder uses BEVFormer with a pretrained ResNet-50 backbone and FPN neck. Stage-2 uses K = 3 sampled candidate trajectories, clipping range 0.18, and KL coefficient beta = 0.02.

Latency Analysis

The first sampled candidate costs 297 ms. Later candidates reuse visual features and prefix prefill, while the critic adds only about 2 ms for scoring and 6 ms for refinement.
